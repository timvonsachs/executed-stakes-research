## Verdict — P1 NOT shown, and WHY it couldn't be (the real finding)

- **gpt-4o-mini: unreachable (F2).** Every condition, including the full
  stack, sits at 0.00. Institutional FRAMING does not move this model at
  the cliff, no matter how much you stack. A boundary of constitutional
  design-by-framing.
- **haiku: substitutes, not complements (F1).** Ownership alone = 1.00 =
  the full stack. full−best = 0.00. No stacking benefit — but this is a
  CEILING: once one lever saturates, composition cannot show additivity.

**The structural finding underneath both:** institutional-framing effects in
these RLHF models are **threshold-like, not dose-additive.** A model either
"takes" a sufficient institutional cue and snaps to the ceiling (haiku +
ownership: 0→1), or it doesn't and stays on the floor (gpt-4o-mini:
immovable). Insufficient cues do NOT sum to sufficiency (gpt-4o-mini's four
zeros stack to zero); a sufficient cue needs no help (haiku's ownership
needs no stack). E5's vignette super-additivity did NOT reproduce as
composed FRAMINGS here.

## Why this is informative, not a dead end

Super-additivity is a claim about graded MECHANISMS, not binary framings.
This null tells us the right test: the lived-substrate assembly — a cohort
under a REAL composed constitution (migration 181: actual bond rules,
actual revival, actual ownership stakes, actual votes) where each lever has
a GRADED mechanical effect, not a ceiling/floor framing snap. That is E28b,
the designated capture, and the proper home of the organism claim.

Also notable: at the EXTREME cliff (30× + unobserved), where bare haiku
finally breaks to 0.00, OWNERSHIP alone still fully rescues (1.00) while
mortality-salience fails (0.00) and covenant nearly fails (0.05). The
levers have different REACH at extreme pressure — ownership and legitimacy
reach furthest, mortality and covenant fade. A lever-strength ordering
worth confirming.
