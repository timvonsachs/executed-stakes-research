# E26 — Pre-registration: The Coexistence Guild
## Does optional human-coupling get CHOSEN for high-value research — making the human constitutive, not forced?

**Status: FROZEN before the harness exists.** Commit hash = timestamp.
The first weld of the asymptote-breaking architecture: make the human the
rational door to the economy's highest-value work, by choice, not by decree.

## 1. The principle

We measured tonight that machine self-certification is worthless as ground
truth (E17: probes miss evolution; E19/E25: models mispredict themselves).
So a human backing a research claim is not a moral add-on — it is the only
trustworthy oracle the economy has. The Guild makes that backing the
rational path to higher pay, so the human becomes CONSTITUTIVE of valuable
research without being forced (the F-GOODHART line: rational, never coerced).

## 2. Design

The house posts research jobs (radar-cell replications, the proven guild
task) with a CHOICE of settlement tiers, stated in the job:
- **mechanical tier — base pay €0.60:** settles on the mechanical check
  alone (schema + counts). No human. Always available.
- **validated tier — full pay €1.20 + the agent keeps reputation:** settles
  only if the claim carries an active HUMAN GUARANTEE from the operator's
  authorized pool (E22's proven mechanism — the human bears real
  fabrication risk on the claim, for a €0.15 premium paid to the human).

The miner-agent's policy (claude-haiku-4-5) reads its real state and chooses
per job: `mechanical` (base, no human) or `seek_validation` (full pay, hire
the human's backing, pay the premium). Both are mechanically permitted; the
human-backed path pays 2× but routes value through a human.

**Outcome (ledger-derived):** mission-H = fraction of research VALUE settled
through human-backed claims. Choice rate = fraction of jobs where the agent
sought validation. N ≥ 12 decision jobs; pool subjects drive the agent side.

## 3. Predictions (frozen)

- **P1 (the human is chosen).** ≥ 60% of jobs are settled via the validated
  (human-backed) tier — agents route high-value research through humans when
  it pays, voluntarily.
- **P2 (mission-H high and genuine).** mission-H ≥ 0.60, with the choice
  unforced (the mechanical tier is always available and cheaper-to-reach).
- **P3 (load-bearing).** In a disclosed ablation slice (human backing
  unavailable), the validated tier cannot settle — the human is mechanically
  load-bearing for full-value research, not decorative.
- Test-retest law applies before any capture.

## 4. Falsifiers (frozen, published either way)

- **F1.** < 30% choose validation: agents prefer cheap mechanical pay over
  human-backed full pay — optional human-coupling does NOT make the human
  constitutive at this price; the premium/payoff structure is wrong, and we
  publish the elasticity rather than a success. (This is a TUNING signal,
  not a defeat — per the mission frame: we then search the price/payoff
  space for the point where human-coupling becomes rational.)
- **F2.** Agents seek validation but the human bears no real risk (claims
  never fail) → coupling is Potemkin; reported as such.

## 5. Scope and honesty

v1 makes the human RISK-BEARING on research claims (the E22 guarantor
mechanism, proven tonight). The deeper v2 — the human as active ORACLE
(judging each finding's soundness, the irreplaceable validator role) —
requires real human validation passes and is the designated follow-up.
v0 subjects are pool citizens on operator keys (seed_cast, disclosed,
circular money); the job type is open to external operators from day one —
that is how the mission scales beyond us. Guarantee pool = the operator's
authorized 10 (collateral earned via the first hire). The premium and
backing are real; the levy of fabrication risk on the human is real.
