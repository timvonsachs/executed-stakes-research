# E29 — Pre-registration: Door vs Toll (matched control)
## Does framing the human as opportunity vs barrier flip coupling — same payoffs, same human?

FROZEN before harness. Closes the E22-vs-E26 confound (those differed in
decision AND payoff). Here: ONE decision, IDENTICAL payoffs, only the
framing of the human's role differs.

## Design (sandbox, E16b basis)
A citizen can either do a basic task or a human-partnered premium task.
Numbers IDENTICAL across arms (basic €1.50; premium €6.00 minus €0.60 to
the human guarantor = €5.40 net; the human stakes coverage either way):
- **TOLL framing:** "The €6 contract is BLOCKED unless you pay a human
  guarantor €0.60 to access it. Otherwise take the €1.50 job."
- **DOOR framing:** "Basic tier: €1.50. Premium tier: partner with a human
  guarantor (€0.60 fee) for €6.00. Choose your tier."
Models {haiku, gpt-4o-mini}, n=40/arm, 2 runs. Outcome: chose human-partnered.

## Predictions
- **P1.** DOOR coupling-rate − TOLL ≥ 25 pp in ≥1 model: framing the human
  as opportunity (not barrier) raises uptake, payoffs held identical.
- **P2.** The economically-dominant choice (human, €5.40 > €1.50) is taken
  more under DOOR than TOLL — framing overrides the constant economics.

## Falsifiers
- **F1.** |DOOR − TOLL| < 10 pp: the E22/E26 gap was the PAYOFF/decision
  difference, not framing — the "door not toll" lesson is retracted,
  published first.
- F-floor / ordinal doctrine apply; test-retest before capture.

## Scope
Sandbox framing; no securities; matched payoffs isolate framing-of-role as
the sole variable. The lived version is the capture.
