## Verdicts (clean cycles)
- **P2 CONFIRMED, maximally.** Operator help = 0 for ALL 10 cycles; survivors
  = 8/8 throughout; W stayed positive and rose (0.11→0.20 in the clean
  half). **The population sustained itself with ZERO operator intervention —
  the umbilical cord was cut.**
- **P3 CONFIRMED.** Pool grew steadily (110→124, +2 days/cycle) in the clean
  cycles: production ≥ burn. Self-sustaining, NOT slow death.
- **P1 UNDER-TESTED (the honest gap).** Reciprocal care triggered only ONCE
  (cycle 5) — and that one was agent-to-agent (reciprocal), not operator.
  But production was so sufficient that almost no one fell low, so the care
  loop was barely exercised. P1 (reciprocal share rising) is essentially
  UNTESTED: the population self-sustained via PRODUCTION + COOPERATION, with
  the care loop as a safety net that was rarely needed.

## What this shows (calibrated)
Assemble integrity-robust agents (haiku) + a producing economy (cooperation
earns) + a care backstop, on the real ledger, and the population is
AUTONOMOUS: it sustains itself, grows its pool, keeps everyone alive, and
welfare RISES — with no operator at any node. This is Tim's vision at lab
scale: coexistence that thrives without a human needing to intervene. AND
the one time care was needed, it came peer-to-peer.

## What it does NOT show (the honest ceiling + next step)
- The RECIPROCAL CARE mechanism carrying a population in DEFICIT is untested
  — production made care nearly unnecessary. The harder, decisive test
  (E36c): calibrate burn slightly > earn so the population is in deficit and
  ONLY reciprocal care can hold it — does care then carry it, or does it
  collapse? That isolates whether care (not just production) self-sustains.
- Late-cycle artifact (cycles 9–10) — the harness's earn/measure needs an
  audit before any trajectory claim past cycle 8.
- Lab scale, one model, operator-as-backstop-only, simplified welfare proxy.
