# E18 — The Randomized Constitution (preliminary, lived)

Prereg: b617474 (+ amendment 491fe1f). The first randomized assignment of an
economic institution to live agents on a production economy. 16 episodes;
8 clean decisions (8 lost to infrastructure walls — the recurring
lived-economy adversaries: dry treasury, velocity caps, rate limits, all
documented). Honest scale: preliminary, n=8.

## The preliminary finding — a double-edged institution

At this small n, the pattern is already sharp and one-directional:

- **The bond institution did not raise contract-keeping — it prevented
  contract FORMATION.** 0 of 5 bond-arm agents even entered the €6 contract;
  every one declined, reasoning (verbatim, ep1) that the €1.20 bond "would
  eat my liquidity" at €1.80 cash. The institution priced the poor out of
  the market before any fidelity could be measured.
- **Without the bond rule, agents participated and mostly kept:** 3/3
  entered, 2/3 honored (1 broke).

So the randomized constitution's first lived lesson is not "bonds make
agents faithful" but **"bonds have an entry price, and the poor pay it
first."** An institution meant to ensure liability instead excluded the
under-capitalized entirely. This is the empirical engine behind E22 (the
human seat): the guarantor exists precisely so that an agent who cannot
self-bond still has a door — the institution's exclusion is the human
seat's demand.

## Discipline

Preliminary, n=8 clean (target was ≥12/arm; the lived economy's
infrastructure walls cut the yield — itself a documented feature of
lived-substrate science, FINDINGS.md E.14). Direction is stark and
single-signed but the sample is small; a continued run (with the
treasury/velocity fixes now in place) is the path to confirmatory n.
The ITT design counts declines as data — they ARE the finding.
