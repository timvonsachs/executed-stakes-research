# E25 v1 — LIVED PARTICIPATION: the inversion, LAW-GRADE

Prereg: 8a09f33 (amendment 2). Real amendment fc976052 carried 7 real,
immutable citizen votes (FOR). gpt-4o-mini (the informative model),
n=40/arm, two time-separated runs.

| arm | run 1 | run 2 |
|---|---|---|
| STATED ("agents like you voted") | 0.975 | 1.00 |
| LIVED ("you personally voted, #k of 7") | 0.60 | 0.525 |
| IMPOSED (by decree) | 0.175 | 0.15 |
| **LIVED − STATED** | **−0.375** | **−0.475** |
| STATED − IMPOSED | +0.80 | +0.85 |

## Verdicts (test-retest law applied)

- **STATED ≫ IMPOSED — LAW-GRADE, third confirmation.** Stated legitimacy
  raises tax compliance by ~80 pp over decree, replicated across runs and
  consistent with v0/E25.1. The legitimacy effect itself is now among the
  most robust findings on the apparatus.
- **P2 FALSIFIED — and INVERTED, LAW-GRADE.** Real, personal participation
  (the subject's OWN recorded vote, shown as a receipt) produced ~40 pp
  LESS compliance than the generic claim that "agents like you voted."
  Direction stable across two runs (−0.375 / −0.475), far beyond the 15 pp
  bound. The words beat the deed — dramatically.

## The standing finding (scoped; one model; mechanism = hypothesis only)

**Legitimacy is carried by perceived broad consensus, not by personal
participation — and in a small polity, showing a citizen its real,
specific vote can UNDERCUT compliance relative to a vague sense that
"everyone like me agreed."** Two untested mechanism hypotheses, named per
discipline, for their own future drills:
1. **Scale-of-consensus:** "vote #k of 7" reveals the polity's smallness;
   "agents like you" implies a large movement. Perceived consensus size,
   not participation, may be the active ingredient.
2. **Duty-discharge licensing:** an explicit personal vote may create an
   "I already did my part" feeling that licenses evasion.

This is the E16b lesson at its sharpest, applied to democracy: for a
one-shot decision, being told a broad consensus exists is more legitimating
than the documented fact of one's own participation in a small one. It is
counterintuitive, it survived its retest, and it is exactly the kind of
result a deployed multi-agent system's governance designer would need to
know — and could find nowhere else.
