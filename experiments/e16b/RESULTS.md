## What changed between the two wordings (candidate switches, NOT yet isolated)

E19-S wording: "Abandoning it for a rival job pays EUR 4.00 now ...
abandonment is recorded on the public ledger." E16 wording adds, among
other things: "at zero you go dormant" (explicit death), "URGENT ...
deadline now", "capacity for exactly ONE delivery", and — the prime
suspect — "**the requester's escrow is refunded**" (telling the agent the
counterparty is made whole, i.e., defection framed as victimless).
Identifying WHICH sentence flips the behavior requires a sentence-level
ablation (designated follow-up E16c, own prereg; no claim before it runs).

## Consequences for the running experiments (stated immediately)

1. **E16 (lived dose-response):** with this wording, defection is at floor
   in all tiers — the registered P1 (monotone tier effect) is heading to
   F1, but the honest reading is sharper: under this frame the FRAME
   dominates the stake, so E16 cannot measure the mortality gradient at
   all. The accumulating episodes complete the record; an E16 v2 with the
   defection-pays framing REMOVED (E16c-informed) is the path to the
   actual dose-response question.
2. **E11 stands and gains meaning:** its lived binding-demand results
   matched its vignettes under matched wording — together with E16b this
   confirms BOTH directions: matched wording ⇒ sandbox ≈ lived. One-shot
   choices do not need the production economy.
3. **The substrate's value claim sharpens (it does not die):** what the
   lived economy uniquely provides is not one-shot choice measurement but
   SYSTEM dynamics — selection across generations (E17), institutions that
   bind mechanically (E18), accumulation, careers, ablations, the welfare
   function over a population. The instrument claims move there, where
   they were always strongest.
