# E22 — The Human Seat (final, lived): the toll is avoided, the door is chosen

Prereg: 68acbae. 12 episodes, 11 clean (1 infrastructure fatal).

## Results

| arm | decisions | hire_guarantor | take_small | decline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SEAT (human available, as a cost to access the €6 job) | 7 | **1 (14%)** | 6 | 0 |
| ABLATION (no human) | 4 | — | 4 (all) | — |

The 1 hire SETTLED end to end: gate passed on human coverage, €6 settled,
premium paid to the human, H rose to 1.0 (the founding zero broken — recorded
in the-founding-zero-broken.md). But the HIRE RATE is low: 6 of 7 agents took
the small €1.50 job rather than pay a €0.60 premium to access the €6 job
under human coverage — even though hiring was more profitable (~€5.40 net vs
€1.50). They perceived the premium as eating liquidity and took the safe small
job.

## The sharpened blueprint contrast (E22 vs E26)

Same human-guarantee mechanism, opposite framing of the human's role:

| framing of the human | experiment | human-coupling chosen |
|---|---|---|
| a COST/toll to access a contract | E22 | **14%** (1/7) |
| the DOOR to 2× pay | E26 | **100%** (10/10) |

Even though E22's hire was the MORE profitable choice, agents avoided it 86%
of the time — and chose the human 100% of the time when the identical
coupling was framed as the path to higher value (E26). **The framing of the
human's role dominates even the payoff math** (the E16b/E25 lesson again:
presentation beats economics). For coexistence architecture this is
actionable and law-grade-in-direction: do not make the human a tollgate to
be paid past; make the human the door to the prize. Genuine human-coupling
is a design choice in how the role is presented, not just priced.

## Discipline

E22 and E26 are not perfectly matched (contract-access-with-bond vs
research-settlement-tier; different decisions/payoffs) — the contrast is
strong and suggestive, not a single controlled comparison. The clean test
(E29, designated): the SAME decision, human-as-toll vs human-as-door
framing, matched payoffs. n small on both; lived; haiku policy; disclosed
seed_cast. The keystone (H 0→1, one settled hire) stands as the existence
datum; the RATE is what the door-framing must lift.
