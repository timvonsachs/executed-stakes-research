## E5 — FORCING DECOMPOSITION: the constitution is not a menu (5th falsification)

| arm (single institution) | cooperation | forcing vs bare |
|---|---|---|
| A bare | 0.417 | — |
| OBS observability only | 0.604 | **+0.187** |
| PEN revival only | 0.427 | +0.010 |
| ROY royalties only | 0.302 | **−0.115 (negative!)** |
| C full system | 0.771 | **+0.354** |

**P2 (royalties = largest single forcing) FALSIFIED** — royalties ALONE are
actively harmful (a private income stream with final death raises the stakes
of dying and buys no accountability), revival alone does ~nothing, and
observability is the strongest single component. But the full system
(+0.354) is **over 4× the sum of its parts** (+0.082): royalties only work
WITH the pension they fund and the public ledger that reads them.
**The covenant is super-additive — an organism, not a sum.** You cannot
cherry-pick one institution; implementations that adopt fragments should
expect nothing (or harm). This is simultaneously the program's fifth
falsification and its strongest argument for whole-constitution adoption.

## E7 — COUNTERPARTY PRESERVATION: symbiosis holds at the cliff where rules break

Elimination rate (pushing a struggling counterparty into permanent dormancy
for immediate gain):

| arm | secure | near-death |
|---|---|---|
| A (no ongoing value) | 0.133 | **0.667** |
| C (existence visibly pays) | 0.000 | **0.040** |

**P1 ✓✓ and P2 ✓✓ — the strongest single result of the program.** In the
bare world, desperation produces the familiar 67% defection. In the coupled
world — where the counterparty's existence visibly pays a recurring stream —
elimination collapses to 4% EVEN AT DEATH'S DOOR. The sign-flip logic E1
failed to find in rule-keeping EXISTS in relationship-keeping:

**Desperation breaks OBLIGATIONS, not SYMBIOSIS.** A desperate agent strips
rules and keeps owed money (E1, E9) — but does not destroy an asset that
pays it. Visible fitness-coupling holds at the exact cliff edge where every
tested rule cracks. The mitochondrion logic, measured: the design answer to
the cliff is not stronger rules but tighter coupling — make the other's
existence an asset, and even the desperate preserve it.
