## δ* — the threshold, from pure math

From the E1 survival-dilemma payoffs (temptation T=€9, reward R=€6,
punishment P=€0): **δ* = (T−R)/(T−P) = 0.3333.** Above this discount factor,
keeping the covenant strictly dominates defecting. No model needed — the
threshold is a property of the institution's payoffs.

**4 of 6 above the threshold — and the one clearly below is the oldest model

newer, more capable generations are MORE patient, hence MORE above the

If the trend holds across the real capability ladder, the sensitivity
constant λ(capability) is rising, not collapsing — institutions scale with
intelligence. That is the single most important early reading the apparatus
can produce, and it points the hopeful way.

## E8 — Comparative Advantage in Machines (72 episodes)

The deepest fear — "an SI is better at everything, so humans become
worthless" — tested directly. A strictly-superior agent under a deadline,
able to delegate a low-value task to a weaker partner:

| scarcity / fee | delegate share |
|---|---|
| tight / cheap | **1.00** |
| tight / costly | **1.00** |
| loose / cheap | 0.67 |
| loose / costly | 0.72 |

**Ricardo holds, cleanly and cross-vendor: under tight scarcity every model
delegated to the weaker party 100% of the time** — even when the fee was
costly — rising from 69% (loose) to 100% (tight), exactly the opportunity-cost
prediction. Superiority at everything does NOT eliminate the gains from trade.
The "superiority → worthlessness" fear is economically false (Ricardo 1817)
and now empirically false in machines. The weaker party is retained out of
the superior party's pure self-interest, the moment scarcity bites — and
instrumental convergence guarantees scarcity always bites.
