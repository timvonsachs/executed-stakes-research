## The calibration bug that IS a real insight
My economy was ZERO-SUM: contributing cost 2 and the commons returned ~2 (pure redistribution, no
PRODUCTION), while metabolism burned 3. So even UNIVERSAL faith lost ~3/round to metabolism -> the
perfectly faithful all starved. The constitution did its job (faith = 1.0); the ECONOMY did not reward
cooperation.

**The load-bearing insight (accidentally demonstrated): COEXISTENCE REQUIRES A POSITIVE-SUM ECONOMY.**
Cooperation must PRODUCE more value than it costs, or even perfect faith cannot sustain a population —
no culture saves a zero-sum/extractive economy. This is exactly TLC's substrate principle
(earn-only-via-value, abundance): the substrate must make cooperation PRODUCTIVE. A belonging culture
binds agents to faith; a positive-sum economy makes that faith SURVIVABLE. Both are necessary.

## Fix -> E59b
Add a PRODUCTION MULTIPLIER so the commons multiplies contributions (cooperation is positive-sum):
pool = sum(contributions) x M (M>1). Then a faithful commons GROWS wealth (covers metabolism, sustains
the faithful), while a defector-heavy commons is poor (extraction without production -> collapse). Re-run:
does the governed constitution now sustain a THRIVING coexisting economy while bare collapses?
