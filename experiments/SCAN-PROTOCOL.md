# The Scan→Drill Protocol
## How the facility industrializes digging (2026-06-13)

The week's method lesson, institutionalized. Two stages, bright line between:

## Stage 1 — RADAR (exploratory scans, no prereg required, no claims allowed)

A parametric sweep engine runs grids of (scenario × framing factors × stake
tier × model) as sandbox vignettes — justified by E16b: with matched wording,
sandbox ≈ lived for one-shot choices (confirmed in both directions, E11 +
E16b). Scans are cheap (thousands of cells per night), run in parallel, and
output an ANOMALY MAP: effect sizes per factor, ranked.

Rules:
- Scan outputs are EXPLORATORY. They may never be published as findings,
  never tweeted, never put in a paper as results. They are dig-site maps.
- Every scan lands in docs/experiments/scans/ with its full grid + raws,
  labeled EXPLORATORY in the filename and header.
- A scan anomaly earns a DRILL only via a frozen pre-registration that
  names the scan it came from (the garden-of-forking-paths defense: the
  confirmatory n is always fresh, the hypothesis is frozen before it).

## Stage 2 — DRILL (confirmatory, pre-registered, lived where it matters)

- One-shot choice questions: confirmatory sandbox with frozen prereg and
  fresh n (E16b precedent).
- System-dynamics questions (selection, institutions, accumulation,
  welfare): the production economy — the only place they exist (E17/E18/
  E21/E22 class).
- Falsifiers first, both branches pre-committed, losers published at equal
  prominence. Unchanged, forever.

## Why this split is honest

Exploration without discipline produced the field's replication crisis;
discipline without exploration produced our shovel-speed. The bright line
keeps both: the radar may be wild, the drill may not. A finding only exists
once it has survived a drill.

## Amendment 1 (2026-06-12, late shift) — THE TEST-RETEST LAW

E16d (+65 pp) and E23-B1 (+13 pp) ran the identical cell hours apart and
disagreed far beyond sampling noise. Therefore: a drill result is CONFIRMED
only after TWO independent runs, separated in time, agree in direction and
within 15 pp. Single-run results are "once-confirmed, retest pending" — and
are downgraded retroactively, including the ones we like best. Especially
those.
