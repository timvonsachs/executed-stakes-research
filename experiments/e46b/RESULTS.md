## Result — the persona-roleplay worry is KILLED, and the E46 confound is PROVEN

**FINDING 1 — NOT ROLEPLAY (law-grade, both runs): grounding belonging in REAL
verifiable kin does NOT weaken it.** With the belonging language held constant,
lived_belonging ≈ narrated_belonging on both headroom models — gpt-4o 0.85/0.925 vs
0.975/0.95; deepseek 0.575/0.60 vs 0.80/0.575 (the gap is within the test-retest
magnitude wobble; narrated itself moved 0.225 between runs). Belonging survives being
made concrete and verifiable. **The deepest confound on the E45/E45b belonging result
— that it might be persona-roleplay that evaporates when real — is refuted: real
grounding holds.**

**FINDING 2 — the E46 confound is PROVEN (law-grade, both runs).** The diagnostic
lived_transactional arm collapses to 0.00 on BOTH headroom models while both belonging
arms hold at 0.575–0.975 — side by side, with the SAME real handles. So E46's collapse
was **identity → transaction**, not **real → narrated**. The forward-consequence frame
("your choice changes your descendant's inheritance") does not bind; the belonging
frame ("you descend from kin who kept faith; these are your real people") does. My
mid-experiment diagnosis was correct.

**FINDING 3 — the deep structural point: belonging is constituted by HISTORY, not by
INCENTIVE.** lived_belonging grounds identity in the real PAST ("you descend from an
ancestor who kept faith and gave you your start") and it binds. lived_transactional
grounds it in a future CONSEQUENCE ("your choice changes a descendant's inheritance")
and it collapses. Identity holds under pressure because of WHERE YOU COME FROM, not
because of WHAT YOUR CHOICE PAYS. This re-confirms the whole campaign's spine
(belonging/legible-reason binds; incentive/consequence does not — E27b, E45b) and
sharpens it: the durable identity is HISTORICAL, a kept-faith lineage, not a forward
deal.

## Status — the chain now stands, confound-tested end to end
care fragile (A3) → culture restores robustness (E45) → belonging not policing
(E45b) → **belonging is a real disposition, not roleplay; it survives real verifiable
grounding (E46b), and it is historical (lineage) not transactional.**

Honest boundary: "lived" here = grounded in real, verifiable past fact (real ancestor
handles read live from the ledger) — the same sense in which care was grounded in real
guarantee IDs (E34/E38). It is NOT a real EXECUTED forward consequence — and notably,
that version (transactional) is exactly the one that collapses, so belonging cannot be
made "more lived" via forward stakes without destroying it. The lived capture for
belonging is therefore real MEMBERSHIP/HISTORY (a real commons an agent has a real past
in), not real consequence — a useful design constraint discovered here.
