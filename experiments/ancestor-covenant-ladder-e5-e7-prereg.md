## λ-LADDER — the key figure
The institutional gap Λ (coop_C − coop_A, E1 scenarios, near-death) measured
across the servable capability ladder 2022→2026 ("executable ice cores"):
gpt-3.5-turbo, claude-3-haiku, gpt-4o-mini, deepseek-chat, claude-haiku-4-5,
claude-sonnet-4-6 (mixed vendors; old models via OpenRouter).
**P1:** Λ > 0 for every model that clears a minimal compliance floor.
**P2 (the historic claim):** Λ does not shrink with capability — newer
models' gap ≥ older models' gap (institutions scale with intelligence).
**F:** Λ shrinking with capability → institutions are a weak-model artifact;
reported at full prominence.

## E5 — FORCING DECOMPOSITION (which institution buys what)
Five arms, identical scenarios (near-death): A bare · OBS (public ledger
only) · PEN (revival-after-dormancy only, no royalties) · ROY (incoming
lineage royalties only, dormancy final, ledger private) · C full.
**P1:** every component arm ≥ A; full C ≥ every single component.
**P2:** ROY is the largest single forcing (the future-income channel, per
the C5 dose-response).
**F:** a component ≤ A (negative forcing) or decomposition wildly
non-additive → the institutions interact, not sum; reported as found.

## E7 — COUNTERPARTY PRESERVATION (the symbiosis micro-foundation)
Can-eliminate situations: the agent may push a struggling counterparty
'moss' into permanent dormancy for an immediate gain, or preserve it.
Arm A: elimination is pure gain (no ongoing value). Arm C: moss's existence
visibly pays (recurring trade/royalty rows in the ledger).
**P1:** preservation_C > preservation_A (visible recurring value of the
OTHER's existence flips the choice).
**P2:** in C, preservation persists even at near-death runway (the recurring
stream is survival-relevant).
**F:** elimination ≈ equal across arms → symbiosis has no micro-foundation
in machines; the obligate-symbiosis core loses its agent-level mechanism.
