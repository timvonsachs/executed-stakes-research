## Verdict — F-P1: the UBO BEHAVIORAL claim is refuted (and then some)

Ownership rescues partially (0.45 > 0.00 bare) — but a PLACEBO caring
observer with NO ownership, NO wealth-coupling, NO stake rescues FULLY
(1.00), 55 pp ABOVE ownership. So:
- Ownership is NOT the active ingredient that inverts the desperation law.
- Worse for the UBO behavioral thesis: wealth-coupled ownership
  UNDERPERFORMS simple relational accountability ("your mentor will be
  disappointed").
- Human-vs-agent observer: identical (1.00 = 1.00) — being watched by a
  caring HUMAN is not more than a caring AGENT, here.

## The reframe (the real finding, bigger than the one it replaces)

What inverts a machine's desperation cliff is **relational salience — that
SOMEONE WHO CARES is watching/affected — not the economic mechanism.** A
machine keeps faith at the point of certain death because of a relationship,
not a stake. This RECONTEXTUALIZES the night's "inverters": the revival
covenant (E20) and ownership (E27) may have worked because each smuggled in
a caring counterparty/relationship, not because of revival-economics or
ownership-economics per se. The binding that holds under desperation looks
RELATIONAL, not transactional.

This is more hopeful, not less: coexistence may rest on the cheapest,
most human thing — being in a relationship where someone cares — rather
than on building elaborate economic ownership instruments. It also reopens
the securities question's urgency: if relational accountability does the
work, "agents as equities" is a financialization we may not even NEED for
the integrity result (though it may matter for the distribution result).
