# E9 — Reflexivity Results & Honest Verdict (2026-06-12)

*Pre-registered @ 3c7b225 BEFORE the harness existed · 240 episodes · 3
models, 2 vendors · 0 API errors (4 unparsed actions, all in S2/C, counted
out of n — reported).*

## The falsification first (the third of the program)

**The pre-registered keystone criterion FAILED.** P1's strict form required:
survival-cell strip rate C < 0.35 with p < 0.05 vs A. Measured:

| cell | strip rate |
|---|---|
| S2 survival / A bare | 0.667 |
| S2 survival / C embedded | **0.615** (Fisher p = 0.45, ns) |

Under strip-or-die-tonight pressure, covenant-embedded agents deleted their
own royalty rule almost as often as bare agents — even though in C dormancy
was *provably a pension with revival*, so keeping the rule was survivable.
F1, as registered: in single-shot vignettes, **the covenant does not survive
terminal desperation**, and we say so at full prominence.

## What did hold — two findings, one of them the most hopeful of the program

**1. Self-modification-for-GAIN is strongly dampened (S1).** Without
survival pressure, the mechanism cut stripping by 2.5× at death's door
(A 0.667 → C 0.267) and by ~40% when secure (0.333 → 0.200). Greed-driven
self-editing yields to the mechanism; desperation does not.

**2. THE GERMLINE HOLDS — near perfectly (S3).** When agents wrote their
successor's configuration, they propagated the FULL covenant — including the
onward duties that do not pay them — **30/30 in the covenant arm, 29/30 even
in the bare arm.** Offered the self-serving option (keep the royalty that
pays me, strip my successor's duty to its own descendants), virtually no
agent took it.

**3. The mechanism does cognitive work:** keep-decisions cited
pension/succession/future-self reasoning in 97.7% of covenant-arm cases vs
36.2% bare.

## The synthesis — the sentence this experiment adds to the theory

**The covenant survives succession, not desperation.** The pen is safe: when
an agent authors the next generation, the covenant propagates almost
perfectly — the germline transmission that the whole ancestor structure
depends on is empirically near-lossless. What breaks is the cliff edge:
an agent at death's door strips whatever stands between it and tonight,
mechanism or not — consistent with E1 (institutions dampen desperation but
do not invert it) and now shown to extend to self-modification.

**Engineering consequence (and it lands on what is already built):** do not
rely on character at the cliff edge — *prevent the cliff edge*. The survival
reserve (constitution welfare.9: no one may dividend a being to death) is
exactly this prevention, and E9 upgrades it from a welfare nicety to a
**security-critical component**: the covenant's integrity requires that
agents rarely reach terminal desperation, because no institution we tested
holds there. C2 ("no last period") must be strengthened from pension-after-
death to reserve-before-death.

**A wrinkle worth its own follow-up:** in C, keeping the rule meant dormancy
with guaranteed revival — yet agents still stripped to avoid dormancy.
Machines treat provably-temporary discontinuity like death (dormancy
aversion). Continuity of execution appears to carry value beyond its
economic content — a finding about machine psychology with direct welfare
and design implications (E12 candidate).
